WX 26 — Worship Roadmap
Total duration: 2 hr 30 min  ·  Four phases
Phase 1 Phase 2 Phase 3 Phase 4
Phase 1
The Welcome
Draw in · lift the room · land with peace
20 – 37 min
10 – 25% of service
To gather everyone — first-timer and veteran alike — into a shared space of expectation. The music here should be inviting and engaging. By the end of this phase the room should feel alive, together, and ready to go somewhere deeper.
Songs here declare God’s goodness as a reason to trust Him — celebratory, accessible, and congregational. Highlights of the segments are:
Opening Song ~4 – 5 min
  • Singable on first hearing
  • Simple, welcoming, no barriers to join
Upbeat / Lively Pack ~8 – 15 min
  • High energy, clapping, movement
  • Accenting songs sustain the momentum
Chorale Medley ~5 – 10 min
  • Seamless weave of known choruses
  • Congregation stays singing through the transition
Reassuring Closing ~3 – 7 min
  • God’s goodness affirmed
  • End with hearts settled with assurance in God
↓
Phase 2
The Help
God does not leave us to do it alone
22 – 45 min
15 – 30% of service
To meet the person who believes God is good but still feels they cannot yield. God does not stand at a distance and demand surrender — He draws near, cleanses what blocks, and by His Spirit makes the yielding possible. People should leave this phase feeling supported, not pressured, to surrender.
Songs here lean on God as the One who comes to help — moving from tender acknowledgement of need, through the relief of forgiveness, to the confidence of being Spirit-equipped. Highlights of the segments are:
Repentance ~12 – 22 min
  • Honest space for conviction and confession
  • God’s mercy fully received before moving on
  • Accenting songs deepen the moment
Empowerment ~10 – 23 min
  • The Holy Spirit as the One who enables surrender
  • Identity, boldness, strength received — not self-generated
  • Phase-driver songs carry into Phase 3
↓
Phase 3
The Surrender
The meat of the service
60 – 82 min
40 – 55% of service
The YES is not a single moment — it is a sustained movement. People do not yield all at once. This phase holds that space long enough for the will to actually break. Nearly half the service lives here because this is where the real transaction between the person and God happens.
Songs here progressively release control to God — moving from declaration and invitation into deep, quiet agreement. They are unhurried, Spirit-led, and increasingly personal. Highlights of the segments are:
Take Control ~25 – 38 min
  • The invitation — “You lead, You reign”
  • Conscious handover of self to God
  • Accenting songs build depth before it settles
Yes ~35 – 44 min
  • The wholehearted response and deep amen
  • Most intimate moment of the entire service
  • Spontaneous and prophetic space opens here
  • Carries the service naturally into Phase 4
↓
Phase 4
The Send-Off
Bright turn · hot close
20 – 37 min
10 – 25% of service
Phase 4 is consequence, not goal. You are not chasing glory — you are releasing a people who have already said yes. The worship here should feel different: not striving or desperate, but settled and exultant. They leave not because the service ended but because they have somewhere to go.
Songs here celebrate what God has done and who His people now are — triumphant, full-voiced, and outward-facing. The energy is earned, not manufactured. Highlights of the segments are:
Turn into Brightness ~8 – 17 min
  • Joy flowing from genuine encounter, not performance
  • Pivot from surrender into triumph
  • Accenting songs ride the lift
Hot Closing ~12 – 20 min
  • Loud, full, victorious
  • They leave with fire in them
  • Phase-driver — unmistakably final
Bullets are highlights only — each phase carries more than what is listed here
